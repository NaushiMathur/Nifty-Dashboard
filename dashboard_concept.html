The Big Picture
What this dashboard does — and why it matters
Most people look at stock prices. This dashboard goes one level deeper: it tracks whether a stock is cheap or expensive relative to its actual earnings. Price alone means nothing. ₹1,000 can be cheap for one company and wildly expensive for another. The key is EPS × P/E × Growth.
📈
Metric 1

Price (daily)

The current market price of one share. Changes every second during market hours. We pull this once at end-of-day for a clean daily snapshot.

Price = Market signal of what investors collectively believe the company is worth right now
🏢
Metric 2

Market Cap

Total value of the entire company. If you wanted to buy the whole company outright, this is what you'd pay. It anchors everything else.

Market Cap = Price × Shares Outstanding
💰
Metric 3

EPS (Earnings Per Share)

How much profit the company made for each share. This is the real number — the actual earnings engine. Price divided by EPS gives you P/E.

EPS = Net Profit (adjusted) ÷ Shares Outstanding
🎯
The Core Ratio

P/E Ratio — The Valuation Signal

How many years of earnings you're paying for today. A P/E of 20 means you're paying ₹20 for every ₹1 of annual profit. Compare this across stocks and sectors.

P/E = Price ÷ EPS  |  TTM P/E uses last 12m earnings  |  Forward P/E uses next year's estimate
🚀
Metric 4

Growth Rate

How fast is EPS growing year-on-year? A company with a P/E of 40 but 40% EPS growth might actually be cheap. Growth justifies premium.

EPS Growth = (EPS this year − EPS last year) ÷ EPS last year × 100
⚡
Metric 5

PEG Ratio

P/E divided by growth rate. PEG < 1 = potentially undervalued. PEG > 2 = potentially overpriced. The single most useful ratio for growth investors.

PEG = P/E ÷ EPS Growth Rate (%)
The Hard Part
Why EPS is tricky — and how we guarantee clean data
Raw EPS from any data source is often dirty. Companies report "exceptional items" — one-time gains or losses — that inflate or deflate EPS artificially. If you use dirty EPS, your entire P/E calculation is wrong. Here's the pipeline we use to guarantee clean EPS.

⚠️ Real Example — Why this matters

Imagine Tata Motors sells a factory and books a ₹5,000 crore one-time gain. Their reported EPS for that quarter shoots up to ₹45. But normally they earn ₹8/share. If you use ₹45 as EPS, the P/E looks like 4× (insanely cheap). If you use the real ₹8, P/E is 22× (more realistic). One wrong number. Completely wrong conclusion.

THE EPS CLEANING PIPELINE →
STEP 1

Raw Reported EPS

Pulled from Yahoo Finance quarterly financials. Includes everything the company chose to report — good and bad.

⚠ Dirty
STEP 2

Flag Exceptional Items

We check if "Exceptional Items" line exists in the P&L. If yes, we record its value (positive = one-time gain, negative = one-time loss).

🔍 Checking
STEP 3

Adjust for Minority Interest

If company owns 60% of a subsidiary, only 60% of that subsidiary's profit belongs to shareholders. We strip out the 40% minority portion.

🔍 Adjusting
STEP 4

Compute Adjusted Net Profit

Adjusted Profit = Reported Profit − Exceptional Items (after tax). This gives us recurring, operating profit only.

🧮 Computing
STEP 5

Clean Adjusted EPS

Divide adjusted profit by shares outstanding. This is the number we trust. Flag it as "ADJ" in the dashboard.

✓ Clean
THREE VERSIONS OF EPS WE TRACK:
TTM EPS
Trailing 12 Months
Sum of last 4 quarters of adjusted EPS. This is what the company actually earned in the past year. Most reliable.
Forward EPS (FY26)
Analyst Estimate
What analysts expect EPS to be next year. Used for forward P/E. Less certain but more forward-looking.
Mid-Quarter EPS
Rolling Average
Between quarterly results, we use the most recent quarter's annualised run-rate or analyst consensus to keep numbers fresh.
The System
How data flows — from Yahoo Finance to your screen
Every day at market close (around 4pm IST), a Python script runs automatically and refreshes everything.
🌐
Source

Yahoo Finance

Free, reliable. Covers all NSE-listed stocks with suffix ".NS". Provides price, volume, shares outstanding, financial statements.

🐍
Engine

Python (yfinance)

A script fetches data for all 50 Nifty stocks in one run. Cleans EPS, computes P/E, PEG, Market Cap, growth rates. Takes ~2 minutes.

💾
Storage

Local Excel / CSV

Results saved to two sheets: Daily Prices (appends each day) and Fundamentals (updated quarterly).

↓
📊
Output A

Interactive Dashboard

An HTML file (or Excel) you open in your browser. Sortable table, charts, valuation signals. Always shows the latest data.

⏰
Output B

Daily Auto-Update

Scheduled to run once per day at market close. No manual work needed. You just open the dashboard and the numbers are fresh.

The Payoff
What you can actually do with this dashboard
Here's what becomes possible once this is built:
🔍
Use Case 1

Spot Undervalued Stocks

Sort by P/E ascending. Any stock with P/E below 15× AND EPS growth above 15% is worth investigating. The dashboard surfaces these instantly.

⚠️
Use Case 2

Avoid Overvalued Traps

P/E of 50× with 8% growth? PEG of 6×? The dashboard flags these clearly so you don't buy into hype-driven pricing.

📉
Use Case 3

Track Market Sentiment

When the index P/E crosses 24×, the market is historically expensive. When it drops below 18×, it's cheap. This gauge tells you where we are every day.

📅
Use Case 4

Quarter-by-Quarter EPS Story

Watch EPS for each company evolve across quarters. Is it growing consistently? Did it spike due to an exceptional item? The history tells the real story.

⚖️
Use Case 5

Compare Within Sectors

HDFC Bank vs ICICI Bank — same sector, different P/E. Which one gives you more earnings for your money? The dashboard answers this in one glance.

🧠
The Real Edge

Clean Data = Better Decisions

Because our EPS is adjusted for exceptional items and minority interest, our P/E ratios are trustworthy. Most free screeners don't do this. That's the edge.
